Practice isn't the bottleneck.
Feedback is.

Uke Can Do It turns ukulele practice into a game. Similar to Just Dance — where hitting the right dance moves earns you points — it tells you which chords to play for your favorite songs and gives feedback via score for how accurately and on time each chord was played.

From strum to score

Four stages turn a raw mic recording into feedback you can act on.

01

Listens

Records your performance through the browser microphone with the MediaRecorder API — start, play, stop.

02

Recognizes

Chroma features are matched against chord templates to tell which chord you actually played.

03

Times

Onset detection and tempo tracking catch every early, late, or missed strum against the target beat.

04

Scores

Each take is scored on chord correctness, timing, and how cleanly each chord was played — with an overall score, a chord-by-chord breakdown, and a visual timeline of mistakes.

How it works

Three steps from picking up your uke to a graded run.

  1. 1

    Pick a song & tempo

    Choose a chord progression and a target BPM. The chords scroll by, Guitar-Hero style.

  2. 2

    Play along on your uke

    Strum each chord in time while the browser records you through the mic.

  3. 3

    Get your score

    Instantly see your accuracy, timing, and a timeline of every mistake.

Prior art & references

The research and commercial systems this project builds on, as cited in our proposal.

Research

  • McFee, Brian, Colin Raffel, Dawen Liang, Daniel P. W. Ellis, Matt McVicar, Eric Battenberg, and Oriol Nieto. “librosa: Audio and music signal analysis in python.” Proceedings of the 14th Python in Science Conference (SciPy 2015), pp. 18–24, 2015. doi:10.25080/Majora-7b98e3ed-003 — our scoring system uses librosa's onset detection, tempo estimation, and chroma features for chord recognition and timing analysis.

Commercial systems

  • Yousician — listens to the player through the device microphone, scrolls upcoming notes and chords across the screen (like Guitar Hero), and gives real-time feedback scored on accuracy and timing.
  • Rocksmith+ — receives input by connecting a guitar over USB or via the computer microphone for acoustic instruments, and analyzes the incoming signal in real time.
  • Simply — tablet/phone app that works across multiple instruments; you play and the device uses mic input to determine if the correct key was played.

Software

  • Librosa — loading recordings, detecting strum onsets, estimating tempo, and computing chroma features for chord recognition.
  • MediaRecorder API — records the performance directly through the browser microphone.
  • FastAPI — connects the frontend to the Python audio-analysis code; the browser sends the recording, the backend returns the score breakdown.
  • NumPy — numerical work such as comparing detected chroma vectors to expected chord templates.
  • React + Vite — the game-style interface where chords scroll by and recording starts and stops.

Evaluation

The full system is compared against an onset-only baseline scorer — one that detects whether a strum happened near each expected chord time but never checks which chord was played — on a self-recorded, hand-labeled dataset of ~20 short ukulele clips containing correct and intentionally incorrect performances (wrong chords, missed strums, late strums, and muted chords).
